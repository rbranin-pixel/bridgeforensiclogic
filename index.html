What BFL does

BFL turns scattered records into a clearer working packet. The work is organizational, documentary, and source-based.

Event Chronologies

Separate complex records into dated, readable sequences by event, admission, claim, issue, or review period.

Source-Document Indexes

Create a reference map showing where key facts, dates, records, bills, letters, or forms appear in the source material.

Missing-Record Checklists

Identify gaps, duplicates, unclear items, and materials that may need to be requested or clarified.

Process

The goal is not to argue the claim. The goal is to make the record harder to misunderstand.

1. Intake: You provide the goal, date range, known issue, and available documents.
2. Separation: BFL separates distinct events, claims, admissions, bills, notices, and record types.
3. Chronology: The record is organized into a dated sequence with source references.
4. Review Packet: You receive a cleaner packet structure for your own review, correction, and use.

Boundaries

BFL is a record-clarity service. It is not a law firm, medical provider, insurer, financial advisor, claims administrator, or expert witness service.

Bridge Forensic Logic does not provide legal, medical, insurance, financial, clinical, or claims advice. BFL does not determine eligibility, diagnose conditions, interpret policy language, guarantee outcomes, or create unsupported facts.

Original records remain the authoritative source material. Clients are responsible for reviewing accuracy, making decisions, and consulting appropriate professionals when needed.
