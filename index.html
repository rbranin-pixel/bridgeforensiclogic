Services

Records made harder to misunderstand.

BFL does not argue your claim for you. BFL separates the events, builds the sequence, indexes the sources, identifies what is missing, and returns a clearer packet.

Record Triage

A first-pass review that separates document types, identifies key dates, and flags obvious gaps, duplications, missing records, or confusing document clusters.

Event Chronology

A date-ordered sequence of events with source-document references, helping distinguish separate admissions, claims, bills, notices, and decisions.

Packet Architecture

A structured claim-support packet with chronology, source index, missing-record checklist, and plain-language administrative summary.

Process

Structure. Sequence. Clarity. Trust.

The work is procedural: preserve the record, reduce confusion, and make the sequence visible.

Receive Client provides records, deadlines, event/date range, known missing items, and the specific administrative goal.
Separate Documents are grouped by medical, insurance, billing, employment, and administrative event.
Sequence Events are placed into a readable chronology with source-document references and visible gaps.
Return BFL returns a clearer packet for client review, correction, and final use.
Boundaries

Record clarity, not legal or medical advice.

Bridge Forensic Logic is not a law firm, medical provider, insurer, financial advisor, claims administrator, or expert witness service.

BFL Does

Organize client-provided records, separate distinct events, build chronologies, index source documents, identify missing or incomplete records, and prepare clearer packet structures.

BFL Does Not

Diagnose, recommend treatment, determine disability, evaluate legal rights, determine claims, guarantee outcomes, contact third parties unless separately agreed, or create unsupported facts.

Client records are used only for the agreed record-clarity service. BFL does not use client records, client facts, or client documents for art, marketing, public examples, or unrelated projects without separate written permission.
